What is TeamPlayer?

TeamPlayer is a group-centered web application that allows you to organize the mess of sharing duties and bills between groups of people in your life, such as friends, roommates, sports teams, and coworkers. And with our calendar-centered interface, each member can stay up to date with their individual tasks and group tasks as well as the amount of money they owe one another. This decreases the need for all members to remember what everyone was responsible for and also decreases the need to remind other group mates to pay you back, saving time from communication and stress from memory.

There are many great features to TeamPlayer:

● Group Management

Create a TeamPlayer group for every group in your life, and all tasks/bills will be centered around these groups. You will no longer have to personally keep everyone up to date with another’s responsibilities. Every member in the group can see what’s going on.

 

● Cycling Task Creation

For the tasks that you seem to always be the only one doing, like cleaning the bathroom or picking up dinner, you can include your groups in your tasks. In addition to setting deadlines, you have the choice to cycle your tasks. This means that when one person has finished it, the task will automatically be given to the next person.

 

● Bill Memory and Bill Splitting

For the outstanding bills people still owe you, and the need to keep track of things that were paid. TeamPlayer compiles expenses paid by and

needed to be paid by users. You can see a summary of what people owe you, and what you owe to others.

 

● Organization

Filters allow you to visualize events for all your groups or just a few -

you choose.

 

Simplify your groups and all the responsibilities in it by using TeamPlayer.

 

 

How do I use TeamPlayer?

TeamPlayer is an interactive website that can be accessed https://team-player.herokuapp.com. Currently, TeamPlayer is only usable through Firefox, but will soon be available on Internet Explorer, Google Chrome, and Safari. Make sure that JavaScript is enabled.

From the above link, TeamPlayer is easy to use!

● Navigate to different locations using tabs:

From these tabs, you can see lists of all tasks you need to accomplish by

date, all bill outstanding payments you owe to other members, all

outstanding payments others owe to you, and all groups you are a part of.

● Click on any task or bill on the calendar or any tab page to see more information about them, edit them, delete them, or mark as done.

● Add tasks from the task page, add bills from the bill page, add groups from the groups page, or add any of these from the home page.

 

See Help to read the User Manual for more information.

 

 

What was the thinking behind TeamPlayer?

TeamPlayer is centralized around a calendar - a group calendar to be exact! It was created with the idea that managing roommates could be easier. Instead of one person being most responsible, assigning chores, making sure items are stocked, paying bills, TeamPlayer can keep everyone accountable, even if it is just knowing that tasks are being done. The calendar is displays all group activity. You know what everyone else has to do, but most importantly, you know what you have to do and when everyone expects you to have finished it.

TeamPlayer can be divided into three parts: tasks, bills, and groups.

 

 

● Tasks are any types of duties: chores, grocery lists, bills to pay. They can contain only one person in the group, or multiple people. They can also be dated and dateless (like a to-do list) to know when they need to be done by.

● Bills are payments that have been done, and can include multiple people, so you can manage how much people owe you.

● Groups are multiple people who share some commonality.

 

 

How do I report a bug?

We love to fix bugs! But before reporting a bug, please see if it is already listed on

our list of bugs. It may not be the exact same question, but could be under a similar category.

If your bug is not listed, follow this link and add a “New Issue” if you have a github account or email us at kronoschronus@googlegroups.com using the following format:

1) Tell us what browser you are using and give us some information about the

bug. It must be clear, such as “Clicking on home takes me to this page instead of that page”, not “the home button doesn’t work.”

 

2) List the steps we need to reproduce this bug. What did you do to reach this

problem? What were you trying to do?

 

3) Give us some contact information (name, email) so that we may ask you

further questions.
