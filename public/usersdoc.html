How do I use TeamPlayer?

TeamPlayer is an interactive website that can be accessed at: https://team-player.herokuapp.com.

  • Navigate to different locations using tabs. From these tabs, you can see lists of all tasks you need to accomplish by date, all bill outstanding payments you owe to other members, all outstanding payments others owe to you, and all groups you are a part of.
  • Click on a day on the calendar to see and complete tasks and bills due that day.
    Or, click on a task or bill within any tab page to see more information about them, edit them, delete them, or mark as done.
  • Add tasks from the task page, add bills from the bill page, and add groups from the groups page.

Click small questions marks on each tab page to see tutorials on how to use each feature, or click "Help" on the web app for FAQ.

Thinking Behind TeamPlayer

TeamPlayer is centralized around a calendar - a group calendar to be exact! It was created with the idea that managing roommates could be easier. Instead of one person being most responsible, assigning chores, making sure items are stocked, paying bills, TeamPlayer can keep everyone accountable, even if it is just knowing that tasks are being done. The calendar displays all group activity. You know what everyone else has to do, but most importantly, you know what you have to do and when everyone expects you to have finished it.


TeamPlayer can be divided into three parts: tasks, bills, and groups.

  • Tasks are any types of duties: chores, grocery lists, bills to pay. They can contain only one person in the group, or multiple people. They can also be dated and dateless (like a to-do list) to know when they need to be done by.
  • Bills are payments that have been done, and can include multiple people, so you can manage how much people owe you.
  • Groups are multiple people who share some commonality.

Working Features

  • Successful log in and log out
    • Upon account creation, the user has a self group so they can create tasks for just themselves. They will not be able to modify this group.
  • Create new groups and add members to them, with confirmation
    • Users can add new members by email, but they will not be displayed until new user makes an account. Until users accept the group invitation, they will be listed as a pending member.
    • When a user is added to a group by another member, they will automatically received this group invitation until an invites list. They can choose to accept or deny the invitation.
  • Adding, Editing and Completing Tasks
    • Users can create tasks for themselves, an entire group, or a small part of an existing group.
    • Tasks can: have or not have a due date, cycle, and repeat.
      • Due dates
          • Tasks with due dates display on your calendar. Tasks without due days display on your todo list.
      • Cycling
          • Tasks can cycle between members. When one is finished, it will be passed on to another.
      • Repeating
          • Tasks can be set to occur on a certain day every week.
      • Note: A cycling task must also repeat!
    • Users can edit tasks they've created.
    • Mark tasks and completed and keep track of tasks within your groups.
  • Adding and Paying Bills
    • Users can create and edit bills only if they were the ones who paid for the bulk of it.
    • Divide up the cost of bills evenly or split individually.
    • Users can see a list of people they owe money to, along with bills that person owes them. A popup displays descriptions of the bill.
    • Users can pay bills they choose to pay, or confirm bills that have been paid to them.
  • Calendar and To Do List Display
    • Tasks and bills for due on a selected day will appear on that day on the home calendar.
    • Tasks and bills for users are colored differently from those for other members of groups they're in.
    • Users can click a specific day to see a list of all events for that day
      • All completed tasks and bills will be grayed
      • Tasks are yellow and will be marked if not yours.
      • Bills you owe others are green.
      • Bills others still owe you are pink.
    • Tasks with no due dates appear on the to do list.
  • User Friendly Tutorials
    • Confused on a page? Click the question mark in the upper right hand corner of the page to see a tutorial popup!
